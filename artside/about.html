Kabeya Ilunga

Afrocentric Artist

Greetings!
Born in 2003 in Goma, in the eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, Kabeya Ilunga is a self-taught artist whose work is rooted in an Afrocentric hyperrealist aesthetic, at the intersection of portraiture, memory, and cultural manifesto. Working with mediums such as charcoal, graphite, pastels, acrylics, and oil paint, he explores the tensions between forgotten traditions, enduring spiritualities, and contemporary African narratives too often marginalized

Shaped by a personal journey marked by displacement, historical fractures, and the invisible forces carried by African and Afro-descendant communities, Kabeya brings face, dignity, and power back to afro identities. After several years dedicated to his second passion software programming he returned to art in November 2023 while living in a refugee camp in Uganda. This artistic rebirth gave rise to "Afrocentrik", a manifesto series of fifteen portraits that draw on ancestral symbols, ritual adornments, and the scars of history to reclaim a vision of the future.
